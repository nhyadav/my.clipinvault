Your Data, Your Device

Privacy Policy

Short version: we don't collect anything. Here are the details.

Last Updated: March 25, 2026

1. What the App Collects

Nothing leaves your machine. ClipinVault stores all clipboard data — text, images, and formatting — in a local SQLite database on your device. There are no servers involved. There is no telemetry. There is no usage tracking. The app works entirely offline.

2. Secret Mode & Encryption

When you mark a clip as "Secret," it gets encrypted using AES-256 before being written to disk. The encryption key is managed by your operating system's credential store — Windows Credential Manager on Windows, or the Secret Service API on Linux. I never see or have access to your keys or encrypted content.

3. What the Website Collects

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. I don't run any analytics, tracking scripts, or cookies. GitHub may log standard server-side data (IP addresses, request timestamps) as part of their hosting infrastructure — that's handled by GitHub's own privacy policy.

4. Third-Party Data Sharing

There is none. I don't share, sell, or transfer your data to anyone. ClipinVault doesn't integrate with any third-party services, ad networks, or analytics platforms.

5. External Links

If you click on links within the app or this website that lead to external sites, those sites have their own privacy policies. I have no control over their content or data practices.

6. Changes to This Policy

If I update this policy, I'll post the changes here with a new date. There won't be any surprises — the core principle (your data stays yours) isn't going to change.

7. Contact

If you have questions about privacy or data handling, you can reach me through GitHub or open an issue on the repository.
